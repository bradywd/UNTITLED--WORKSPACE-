White Clouds

White Clouds

Located near Clayton, Idaho this hike extends through the beautiful yet rugged terrain of the Sawtooth Mountain Range. The trailhead is located at 4174 Big Boulder Crk, Clayton, ID 83227 and is near the Livingston Mill.

The Hike

This hike is an out and back trail that extends 20.8 miles with an elevation gain of over 4000 feet. Average completion takes around 10 and a half hours. Some people chose to complete the entire hike in a single day, but others might choose to make shorten the hike to 14 miles (2200 feet of elevation gain) and make Walker Lake their destination. No matter how far up the trail you chose to hike, this is an excellent trail to pack in and camp for one or even multiple nights. Four beautiful lakes are a fisher’s paradise, but also make for a breath-taking sight for any explorer.

Items to Bring (Dayhike)

1. Backpack

2. Shoes with good traction and support

3. Lots of water

4. Sunscreen (much of the trail is not shaded)

5. Lunch and Snacks

6. Fishing Pole (optional)

Things to Know Before You Camp

The trailhead for this hike is a great place to set up camp if you want to arrive the night before and get an early start the next day. There is an outhouse on the premise but don’t expect the same luxury once you begin your hike. There aren’t necessarily designated camping spots once you make it to the lakes, but some stretches of ground make for a lot better spot to set up a tent than others.

It is important to know that this is Bear Country, and you will need to put all your food items and anything else that smells in a bear bag and hang it up a tree each night as you get ready for bed. Remember that a bear bag should be raised 15 feet from the ground and be about 10 feet away from the trunk of the tree.

Although some of the camping spots might have fire pits, fires aren’t allowed all times of the year. It is important for you to do your research before you start your hike to see whether the National Forest Service is permitting fires in the area while you will be camping.

Items to Bring (Overnight)

1. Backpacking Backpack

2. Shoes with good traction and support

3. Multiple Water Bottles (20 oz Gatorade Bottles are lightweight, strong, and have a large cap which is convenient for filtering water)

4. Water Filter

5. Compact Camp Stove (lighter or matches if stove is not self-lighting)

6. Small Pot

7. Food and Snacks (dehydrated meals work well)

8. Dinnerware Set

9. Sunscreen

10. Tent/Hammock

11. Sleeping Bag

12. Sleeping Pad

13. Camp Pillow

14. Bear Sack/ Strong Garbage Sack

15. paracord for bear bag (25 or more feet)

16. Toiletries

17. Extra Clothing

18. Warm Jacket/Coat

19. Fishing Pole (optional)
